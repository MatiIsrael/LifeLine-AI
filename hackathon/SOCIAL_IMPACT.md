# Social Impact Metrics

## Impact thesis
Minutes saved in the **golden hour** directly correlate with lives saved. Lifeline targets the gap where technology fails today: **rural, elderly, and high-crime corridors**.

## Target outcomes (pilot model)

| Metric | Baseline (typical) | Lifeline pilot target |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Time to first contact | 12–45 min | **−30%** |
| SOS success in dead zones | ~40% | **85%+** (SMS + mesh + queue) |
| Dispatcher situational awareness | Low | **Live GPS + risk overlay** |
| Preventive safety actions | Rare | **Route warnings before entry** |

## Populations served
- **Rural communities** — offline-first, SMS, mesh relay
- **Elderly / fall risk** — on-device fall detection
- **Women & night workers** — panic movement, danger zones, silent SOS
- **Disaster zones** — weak-net mode, coordinator dashboard

## Projected scale narrative (for judges)

> In a city of **2 million**, if Lifeline reaches **5% adoption** (100k users) and prevents **1 delayed response per 10,000 users per year**, that's **10 lives** — per city, per year. At national scale, impact compounds.

## Equity
- Works without premium devices (Flutter, lightweight preview for outreach)
- Multilingual roadmap; plain-language home explainer
- Free tier SOS; government subsidy for vulnerable groups

## UN SDG alignment
- **SDG 3** — Good health and well-being
- **SDG 11** — Sustainable cities (safe mobility, risk maps)
- **SDG 16** — Peace, justice, strong institutions (transparent dispatch)

## Measurement plan
- Pilot KPI dashboard (dispatch time, resolution rate, offline sync success)
- Anonymous aggregate reports — no surveillance marketing
- Third-party EMS evaluation at 90 days
